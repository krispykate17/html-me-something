German

My first foreign language. I chose it in highschool over Spanish and French because I was told it was more difficult and I like a challenge! Besides, I knew if I could master it, then I could always learn other languages later. I spent 2 years following regular coursework. The last 2 years I had schedule conflicts but still wanted to take it, so they allowed me to take it as a dual enrolled student at St. Charles Community College. After that, I continued to try taking courses throughout my undergrad, and came close to having enough credits for a minor, but again, the scheduling just never lined up. Sadly, this led to me losing touch with the language, but I hope to one day be able to pick it back up.

French

I started taking French senior year of highschool. People thought I was crazy for taking more than one foreign language. Honestly, I would've started taking French even sooner if my schedule and counselor would've allowed it. I caught on very quickly to French and it felt more natural to me than German. Not only that, but it worked into my schedule better because more courses were offered in my undergrad... and I eventually switched majors to the point where my undergrad degree is now in Modern Languages with an emphasis in French! I now try to keep up with it by listening to podcasts, music, watching tv, reading news articles, and I try to get to Montreal as often as I can!

Chinese

Naturally, I wanted to learn an Eastern language to pair with my knowledge of English and French. Even though Japanese has always been a dream and goal, the not-fun, logical part of my brain said "Learn Chinese! It will be more useful and you can get a better job!"

Well... I said, "Yolo! Let's go to China and learn Chinese!"

There were a few more steps involved, but that was essentially it. I took a crash course in Chinese at UMSL to prepare for my trip which involved 8 credit hours worth of material mashed into 3 credit hours mashed into 4 weeks.

I was supposed to be in China for a semester... only made it 2 weeks. One day I'll write a book about it. For now, just know it was nightmarish, and I don't know that I'll ever go back.

C++

Ahhh my first attempt at learning a coding language! It was one of the few programming courses offered at UMSL at the time. So why programming?

  1. It's not so far off from learning a human language.
  2. It uses both creativity and logic.
  3. I've taken several personality job assesment skills. Every single one recommended programming. Go figure!

So I decide to give programming a try... and I hated it! I was stuck in a class filled with math and engineering majors who couldn't care less about programming, and were just there because it was required. The teacher knew it too. So I tried my best, but it wasn't a great starting environment and the only joy I got from it was putting silly "Archer" references in my projects so my teacher and I could get a laugh.

Python

Winter 2016, I decided my initial opinion of programming probably wasn't acurate and that I just needed a new environment to learn. Enter in LaunchCode! I saw that they were accepting applications for LC101 and so I applied.

Well, I was right! It makes a world of difference to learn with others who are just as passionate about the subject as you are. Especially, when trying to tackle such an intimidating and huge subject like computer science.

Python was a breeze compared to C++ and I felt the language itself and the way it was presented, really helped me grasp concepts I never would've understood on my own.

Thanks LaunchCode!

Japanese

I believe my love of language/cultures/travel stemmed from the moment my grandparents came back from Japan in 1998. My 5 year old self thought it was the most amazing thing when they came back with silk kimonos, wooden shoes, paper umbrellas, and of course... pikachu earmuffs!!!

I then had a Japanese friend in elementary school who introduced me to the world of Sailor Moon. I was completely hooked on Japanese pop culture after that.

As of right now, I don't know much Japanese, and it's a work in progress. I hope to know enough to travel to Japan by Spring 2018!
